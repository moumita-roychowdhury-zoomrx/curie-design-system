Remote heart monitoring device slashes hospital readmissions by 69%

about 17 hours ago · Medscape

A wireless heart device that monitors pressure in the lungs' blood vessels is proving to be a game-changer for heart failure patients. The CardioMEMS system helped slash hospital readmissions by 69% in a major study of 304 patients across Europe and Australia. The implantable sensor lets doctors adjust medications remotely based on daily pressure readings, keeping patients out of the hospital while improving their quality of life.
