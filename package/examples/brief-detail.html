Lower cholesterol targets cut heart events by one-third in landmark study

about 17 hours ago · 9 Sources
Medscape Professional Heart Daily Healio TCTMD Healio Medscape TCTMD ScienceDaily Medscape

Summary

Targeting LDL cholesterol below 55 mg/dL instead of 70 mg/dL reduced major cardiovascular events by 33% in patients with heart disease, according to the Ez-PAVE trial. The South Korean study of over 3,000 patients used mostly statins and ezetimibe to achieve these aggressive targets.

A groundbreaking study from South Korea is providing fresh evidence that when it comes to cholesterol, lower really is better. The Ez-PAVE trial found that targeting LDL cholesterol below 55 mg/dL instead of the conventional 70 mg/dL reduced major cardiovascular events by 33% in patients with existing heart disease.

The three-year study followed over 3,000 patients with atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, achieving median LDL levels of 56 mg/dL in the intensive group versus 66 mg/dL in the conventional group. Importantly, these aggressive targets were reached primarily using statins and ezetimibe—not expensive PCSK9 inhibitors—making the approach more accessible and cost-effective.

Key Takeaways:

  • 33% reduction in composite endpoint of CV death, heart attack, stroke, revascularization, or unstable angina hospitalization
  • 59% of intensive-target patients still failed to reach their goal despite treatment
  • Study used mostly traditional lipid-lowering therapies rather than newer, costlier options
  • Results align with recently updated American dyslipidemia guidelines promoting earlier, more intensive treatment

Why it matters:

This trial provides the randomized evidence that clinicians needed to confidently pursue more aggressive LDL targets. By demonstrating that substantial cardiovascular benefits can be achieved using widely available medications, the findings could reshape how doctors approach cholesterol management in heart disease patients worldwide.
